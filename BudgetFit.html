"Architectural" picks in-wall or in-ceiling speakers throughout — the clean, cabinet-free look, common for a built-in home theater. "Standard" keeps freestanding/bookshelf speakers.

The engine picks one model per category, upgrading and downgrading until the equipment total lands as close to the budget as possible. Surround and Atmos speaker quantities scale with the channel selection — 7.1 uses two pairs of surrounds, 9.1 uses three; Atmos configs add height speakers on top. In HTiB mode the box covers a 5.1 setup on its own; picking 7.1+ adds an extra surround pair, and picking an Atmos config adds in-ceiling speakers, on top of the box.
